SUMMARY
Dielectric constants and loss factors of various polyacrylic and polymethacrylic esters have been measured for frequencies ranging from .5 to
500 kilocycles/sec (in some cases to I4 megacycles/sec). The measurements
covered a fairly wide temperature interval, both below and above the glass
transition temperature TK.
In all cases a secondary, or fi absorption band was found, which could
not be attributed to cooperative movements of the chain segments. The dispersion curves could not be represented b j the classical equation of Landell,
Williams and Ferry, valid for the u bands. The energy of activation was lower
for the P band than for the U , and for P bands this energy did not change
abruptly at Tg. The p relaxation spectrum contrary to the R one, spreads out
as the temperature is lowered.
The fi band seems to be due to movements of side groups, and when
these are not too bulky the movements are not frozen at Tg.
