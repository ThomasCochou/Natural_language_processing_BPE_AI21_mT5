Summary. The activity of pontine swallowing neurons (PSNs) at the level of the trigeminal (Vth)
motor nucleus were studied in sheep anaesthetized
with halothane.
Electromyographic (EMG) recordings from different muscles innervated by the Vth nerve have
shown that the mylohyoid (MH), the anterior body
of the digastric (Dig) and the medial pterygo~d (Pt)
muscles discharged at about the same time during
swallowing induced by stimulating (short train of
shocks at 30 Hz or 500 Hz) the superior laryngeal
nerve. In contrast, the masseter and temporal muscles remained "silent", indicating that these muscles
were not concerned with reflexly induced swallowing.
Using extracellular tungsten microelectrodes, the
Vth motor nucleus region was explored. The activity
of 193 PSNs was recorded. These neurons have been
classified into two groups:
1) The discharge of Group I PSNs (179 neurons)
persisted after motor paralysis (Flaxedil, 2 mg/kg)
indicating a centrally patterned activity. 123 PSNs
were normally "silent" and activated only when
swallowing occurs. Swallowing activation was characterized by a variable burst of spikes (one to seven
spikes) at low frequency ( - 50 Hz) during the period
of EMG activity of MH, Dig and Pt muscles. These
neurons were identified as a motoneurons supplying
swallowing muscles. 56 were antidromically activated
by motor nerve stimulation (MH-Dig mainly); they
were located in the posterior part of the Vth motor
nucleus (ventro-medial region). Other PSNs (56
neurons) could fire spontaneously. They exhibited a
long lasting swallowing activation (10-35 potentials)
